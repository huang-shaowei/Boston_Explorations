Boston Commons

Oldest park in USA

Boston Common's 44 acres of lush green space form the heart of the city. This is the place where you can enjoy some of the city's most popular activities and events. Walk across Boston Common on a warm day and you'll see 21st century Boston at its best.

Faneuil Hall Marketplace

Historic market complex

Most stores at Faneuil Hall Marketplace are in the North Market and South Market buildings. Faneuil Hall does offer some shopping on the ground floor, but the rest of the building is a historic site. Quincy Market contains a terrific food hall - more about that in a moment.

Museum of Fine Arts

Boston's heritage!

Founded in 1870, the museum moved to its current location in 1909. With hundreds of galleries filled with treasures from the ancient world to contemporary art, Boston's Museum of Fine Arts can easily keep you enthralled for an entire day.

Freedom Trail

Follow the history trail

Walk Into History along the iconic Freedom Trail – the 2.5 mile red line leading to nationally significant historic sites, each one an authentic treasure.Led by 18th-century costumed Freedom Trail Players®, tours feature tales of high treason, mob agitations, revolutionary actions, and partisan fights of the American Revolution.

New England Aquarium

Way to nature's wonderland!

Explore marine life found in the Olympic Coast National Marine Sanctuary.The four-story exhibit features hundreds of Caribbean reef animals.Visitors can reach out and gently stroke sharks and rays. Aquarium is home to more than 80 penguins.Watch seals swim, play and sleep on the Aquarium's Front Plaza.
